Est. 2024 — Accra, Ghana

Three digits. One foundation.

444 Store began as a small capsule of six pieces, sewn by a two-person team in a rented Osu studio. Today it's a considered streetwear label built around one idea: dress like you already know who you are.

Our Story

Built from a number, not a mood board.

444 is an angel number — a signal, in many spiritual traditions, for protection, structure and alignment. Our founders kept seeing it during the year they left stable jobs to cut their first samples on a borrowed machine in East Legon.

The name stuck because it matched the mission: build a wardrobe foundation people could trust completely, the way you trust a sign that keeps showing up when you need it most. No hype-chasing, no disposable drops — just fabric, fit and finishing that hold up in Accra heat and Accra traffic alike.

Every piece is still designed in-house and packed by hand from our studio off Oxford Street, Osu — checked twice before it ever reaches your door.

What Guides Us

Mission, vision & philosophy.

01

Our Mission

To build the most trusted streetwear foundation coming out of Ghana — clothing engineered for real Accra life, sold at prices that respect the people wearing it.

02

Our Vision

A future where "Made in Ghana" is a mark of premium quality on the global stage, worn confidently from Osu to Oxford Street, London.

03

Our Philosophy

Fashion should feel like alignment, not performance. We design for people dressing for themselves first — considered fits over fast trends, always.

How We Got Here

Our Timeline.

2022

The idea takes shape

Two friends, a shared frustration with imported fast fashion, and a first sketchbook of tees named after the number that kept appearing everywhere.

2023

First capsule, six pieces

A limited run of Foundation Tees sold directly through Instagram DMs — the entire drop sold out from a single carousel post.

2024

444 Store officially launches

A full studio in Osu, a proper size run, and our first hoodie — the Alignment Hoodie — which remains our best seller today.

2025

Heritage Edition drops

Our first collection referencing Adinkra symbolism through construction and hardware rather than print — a study in restraint.

2026

444 Vol. II — Guardian & this website

Our biggest collection yet, utility-driven and built for the everyday commute — alongside the digital home you're standing in right now.
